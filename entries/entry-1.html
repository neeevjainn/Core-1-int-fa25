The Sun is the star at the centre of the Solar System. It is a massive, nearly perfect sphere of hot plasma, heated to incandescence by nuclear fusion reactions in its core, radiating the energy from its surface mainly as visible light and infrared radiation with 10% at ultraviolet energies. It is the main source of energy for life on Earth.

The Sun has been an object of veneration in many cultures and a central subject for astronomical research since antiquity. The Sun rotates faster at its equator than at its poles. This differential rotation is caused by convective motion due to heat transport and the Coriolis force due to the Sun's rotation. In a frame of reference defined by the stars, the rotational period is approximately 25.6 days at the equator and 33.5 days at the poles. Viewed from Earth as it orbits the Sun, the apparent rotational period of the Sun at its equator is about 28 days.

In astrology, the Sun represents the conscious ego, the self and its expression, personal power, pride and authority, leadership qualities and the principles of creativity, spontaneity, health and vitality, the life force. The Sun is the planetary ruler of Leo.

The placement of the Sun in your birth chart describes your core identity—the person you are becoming. While the Moon represents your emotions and inner world, the Sun represents the "you" that shines out to the world. It is the engine of your personality, driving your desires, your ambition, and your sense of purpose. A well-aspected Sun grants confidence, warmth, and the ability to lead, while a challenged Sun may struggle with ego, arrogance, or a lack of direction.

Stellar Trivia

Did you know? The Sun makes up a staggering 99.86% of the mass of the entire Solar System. The remaining 0.14% is mostly Jupiter! It consumes about 600 million tons of hydrogen every second, converting it into helium and pure energy.
