Uranus is the seventh planet from the Sun. It has the third-largest planetary radius and fourth-largest planetary mass in the Solar System. Uranus is similar in composition to Neptune, and both have bulk chemical compositions which differ from that of the larger gas giants Jupiter and Saturn. Unique among the planets, Uranus rotates on its side; its axis of rotation is tilted sideways, nearly into the plane of its solar orbit. This results in extreme seasonal variations.

Uranus is the planet of awakening, innovation, and sudden change. It represents the urge for freedom and rebellion against the status quo. It governs technology, electricity, and the future. Uranus breaks structures (Saturn) to make way for the new. It acts as a "higher octave" of Mercury, representing genius and intuition that strikes like lightning. In a chart, Uranus shows where we are unconventional, where we seek independence, and where we might experience sudden disruptions.

Stellar Trivia

Uranus is the coldest planetary atmosphere in the Solar System, with a minimum temperature of 49 K (−224 °C; −371 °F).
